What Does Missing Shingles After Wind Storm Mean in Chapel Hill?

Missing shingles after a wind storm leave your roof exposed and vulnerable to immediate water damage. When shingles are torn away, they expose:

  • Roof decking to the elements - Plywood or OSB sheathing not designed for weather exposure
  • Underlayment barriers - Temporary protection that won't last without shingles
  • Interior spaces to water intrusion - Direct path for rain into your home
  • Surrounding shingles to failure - Wind can continue lifting adjacent shingles

Don't Wait - Act Fast

Missing shingles create immediate vulnerability to water damage. The exposed roof decking will deteriorate rapidly, and any subsequent rain will likely cause interior damage. This is considered an emergency repair situation.

What You Should Do Right Now:

  2. Check for other signs - Look for additional damage
